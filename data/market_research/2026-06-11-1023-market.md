### CRITICAL: the live evaluations disagree because Python fetches stale bars

Today's `## Evaluation 10:08 GMT+2` (journal/2026-06-11.md) reports BTC
`daily regime=uptrend` with `last=75743.69` — that daily close is from
**2026-04-18**, 54 days old. Root cause (verified by direct probe):

- `scripts/run_evaluation.py:182` `_bars_start(buffer=1.6)` + `limit=N` +
  no `sort`/pagination in `get_crypto_bars` (run_evaluation.py:217-239).
  Alpaca returns bars **oldest-first** from `start`, truncated at `limit`,
  so the function returns the FIRST N bars of a 1.6×-wide window:
  - 15-min bars (limit 200): last bar 2026-06-10T02:15Z → **~30 h stale**.
  - 4H bars (limit 120): BTC last bar 2026-05-17 → **25 d stale**, and only
    43 bars returned → 4H signal reported "insufficient 4H history" → score
    component 6 silently 0.
  - Daily bars (limit 90): window 2026-01-19 → 2026-04-18 → **54 d stale**.
- Consequence: the regime gate believes "uptrend" while the actual regime is
  a −25% to −43% 30-day downtrend. The long gate is open and the short gate
  is closed — exactly backwards. All 6-point scores are computed on 30-hour-
  old 15-min bars.
- `scripts/research.py:82` uses `sort=desc` (fresh) and the dashboard
  paginates via `next_page_token` (fresh) — so the three data paths disagree.

### Risk parameters vs. current volatility

- **ATR stops**: sizing uses 15-min ATR (BTC stop_1.5x ≈ $389 ≈ 0.6% of
  price). With stop distance ~0.6%, raw qty = 1% equity / 0.6% ≈ 160% of
  equity → the per-symbol cap always binds, so every entry is cap-sized.
  Realized risk is therefore governed by the 5% hard stop, not the ATR stop:
  a cap-sized BTC position (30%) stopping at −5% loses **1.5% of equity —
  exceeding the 1% risk rule**. Daily-scale volatility (ATR% 4.2–9.5%) also
  means a routine daily swing fully traverses the 5% stop.
- **Correlation budget**: avg pairwise 90-day ρ of daily log-returns = 0.81;
  ρ vs BTC = 0.57 (DOT) to 0.91 (ETH). Max 3 positions across tiers is
  effectively ONE trade — diversification benefit is minimal in this regime.
- **Limit bands**: 7 of 82 order submissions since 2026-05-09 were rejected
  by the band checks — 4 entries (0.2% band) and **3 stop-loss exits (0.5%
  band)**, e.g. AVAX/USD SELL rejected twice on 2026-06-03 before finally
  filling. Rejected stop-losses in a fast tape are a real risk-control gap.

### Strategy fit

The 6-point confluence mixes mean-reversion (BB %b, RSI oversold) with trend
signals. In today's eval every symbol earned BB +1 ("near lower band") while
in mark-down — in a downtrend the lower band keeps walking lower, so this
component systematically pushes the score toward longs. Combined with the
stale uptrend regime reading, the system has been buying dips in a −25%/30d
market, which matches the 21% win rate.

- FAIL — Live edge negative: −4.57% in 33 days, win rate 21%, PF 0.15,
  Sharpe −6.98; walk-forward also negative on all timeframes.
- WARN — 1% risk rule not binding: cap-sized entries risk up to 1.5% equity

- WARN — Avg pairwise ρ 0.81: the 3-position correlation budget provides
  little diversification; treat the book as one beta trade.
- WARN — 3 stop-loss exits rejected by the 0.5% limit band since 2026-06-03.

## Recommendations

1. **Fix the bar fetch immediately** (highest priority): in
   `run_evaluation.py get_crypto_bars`, either add `sort=desc` (then
   re-sort ascending) or follow `next_page_token` until `end`, so the LAST
   N completed bars are returned. Re-run a dry-run evaluation and confirm
   `daily last` matches the live snapshot within one day.
2. Until fixed, treat all evaluation output as unreliable; with fresh data
   the regime gate would currently block all longs and permit shorts only.
3. Reconcile the 1% risk rule: size against the effective stop
   (min(1.5×ATR, 5%)), or compute ATR on a higher timeframe, so cap-sized
   positions cannot risk >1% at the hard stop.
4. Re-run `scripts/walkforward_evaluate.py` after the data fix — current
   negative-Sharpe results may partly reflect the same stale-window fetch.
5. Consider widening the stop-loss band or retrying rejected stop-loss
   orders within the same cycle; a rejected stop in mark-down is unbounded
   risk until the next hourly evaluation.
6. Clear the stale BTC `stop_order_id` (2026-05-29) from
   `data/positions_state.json` — no open orders or positions exist.
